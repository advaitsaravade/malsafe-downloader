Your anti-virus is like a security camera connected to a criminal database.

What is a malware/virus scanner?

Imagine you have a house (computer), and you want to protect its contents from criminals (malware). So you come up with the following idea:

In front of each door, you will put a security camera. It will scan every one that tries to enter the house. The camera is linked to a criminal records database. If it spots someone that is in the database it will send you a text message and prevent them from entering your house.

NEXT SLIDE

What are the problems with this approach?

  1. The criminal database copy can become out of date.
  2. If someone has just started their life of crime, and is not in the database, then it lets them in.
  3. It treats everyone the same: You, your spouse, your kids, friends, neighbors, random strangers, are all let in (if they are not in the database).
  4. People with a mask or paper bag over their head are let in as well.
NEXT SLIDE

Malware can deceive your anti-virus protection by pretending to be legitimate.

Criminals are always one step ahead of modern anti-virus systems, and can invent new ways to deceive your computer's security.

Why should we not rely on such a system?

Criminals can and are always finding ways to trick the camera. This means that our security camera can be tricked and does not always keep criminals from entering our house.

NEXT SLIDE

Is there a better, more secure way?

Instead of a list of people to keep out, have a list of people to let in. Everytime you meet someone you'd trust into your house, add their name to your security system.

Wouldn't you like to have complete control over who you'd allow into the privacy of your home?
